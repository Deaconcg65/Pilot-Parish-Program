The Reality

Most Parish Technology Is a Liability, Not an Asset

Pastors don't go into ministry to manage network switches and software licenses. But right now, in most parishes, outdated and unmanaged technology is quietly creating real risk — security vulnerabilities, compliance gaps, slow systems, and staff who waste hours on problems that should never happen.

The Hard Truth

Catholic institutions are increasingly targeted by ransomware and phishing attacks. An unpatched system, a weak password policy, or an unmonitored network is not a minor inconvenience — it is an open door. A single breach can cost a parish tens of thousands of dollars and months of disruption. Most small parishes have no one who knows this is happening.

🔓

Security Gaps

Unpatched systems, weak passwords, no multi-factor authentication. Phishing attacks land because there's no filter or training in place.

📡

WiFi That Fails

Consumer-grade routers covering a building designed for enterprise equipment. Dead zones, dropped connections, no separation between staff and guests.

🖥️

Aging Hardware

Computers running outdated operating systems that no longer receive security updates. Hardware that fails at the worst possible moment.

📋

No Compliance

Diocesan standards for Microsoft 365 and data handling exist — but without help, most parishes don't know where they stand or how to get there.

💾

No Backup Strategy

Parish records, financial files, and donor data sitting on a single machine with no backup. One failure and years of records are gone.

🆘

No One to Call

When something breaks, staff scramble, wait for a volunteer who knows technology, or pay an emergency rate to a local repair shop — with no continuity of care.
